About me

Translating ideas into Javascript

I love translation. As a linguist, I translate between three languages. As a junior web developer, I am working hard on becoming fluent in Javascript. I enjoy this programming language and try to write code which is well-structured and easy to comprehend. I also do my best to make my coding process efficient, which is why I use frameworks, preprocessors and package managers.

2018
Web development course

I have completed an intensive, 6-month course which covered the development of websites and web applications. I studied independently with the support of a personal mentor.

The curriculum included both front-end and back-end technologies: Javascript (including ES6, React, Redux), HTML5, CSS3 (including its preprocessors and frameworks, such as SASS and Bootstrap), developer tools (including Git, NPM, Webpack), Node.js, Express, databases (MongoDB).

2011-2018
Career in translation and communication

Over the course of my career as a linguist and social media specialist, I have completed numerous projects for IT clients, including Oracle, Google and Intel.

I have had a chance to translate and create website content, manage websites and, in the case of Habitat for Humanity Poland's website, coordinate and oversee the entire process of the website redesign (which involved cooperation with UX designers and programmers).

2006-2011
Studies in linguistics

I have a Master's degree in English Philology earned at the University of Silesia (Katowice, Poland). I completed the English to Polish Translation Program, which involved studying Arabic for 5 years.

My academic experience turned out to be useful when I started coding: classes in descriptive grammar and comparative linguistics gave me a solid basis for understanding the structure of programming languages. Also, I quickly realized that Javascript is pretty easy in comparison with Arabic.
